Backtest fidelity — same-bar resolutions

101 of 704 resolved trades (14%) exited on the fill bar — the same H1 candle that filled the limit also hit SL (and sometimes TP1). On H1 we cannot tell whether TP or SL printed first, so these are booked SL (pessimistic, no look-ahead). Of the losers, 0 had TP1's price touched in-bar yet booked a full −1R. Read the headline knowing this slice is H1-resolution-limited, not clean directional losses — a lower-timeframe fill model would reclassify some.

Trades worth a second look — what the flags mean

365 trades flagged for a second look — the full list is in the Excel Second Look tab. The lessons this run:

  • 20 winners left money on the table — they peaked at +3.92R on average but you booked far less, handing back +2.55R per trade. This is an exit/target lesson: the runner or a farther TP would recover it. Most concentrated on NZDUSD (3t, +0.95R avg).
  • 71 losers ran green first — they reached +0.72R in profit on average before reversing to a full loss, bleeding -69.0R in total. 1 of them showed ≥+1R. This is a stop/break-even lesson: a BE or partial rule would have rescued real R. Worst in the London (30t, -1.00R avg) bucket.
  • 274 high-score setups still lost (avg score 6.1), bleeding -265.8R. The score did not protect these — consistent with the settled finding that the confidence score does not rank outcomes. Concentrated on USDJPY (52t, -1.00R avg).
