Backtest fidelity — same-bar resolutions

16 of 107 resolved trades (15%) exited on the fill bar — the same H1 candle that filled the limit also hit SL (and sometimes TP1). On H1 we cannot tell whether TP or SL printed first, so these are booked SL (pessimistic, no look-ahead). Of the losers, 4 had TP1's price touched in-bar yet booked a full −1R. Read the headline knowing this slice is H1-resolution-limited, not clean directional losses — a lower-timeframe fill model would reclassify some.

Excursion — MFE / MAE (stop & exit tuning)

How far every trade ran in your favour (MFE) and against you (MAE) before it resolved. This is the direct read on whether the stop is too tight, the target too near, or a break-even rule is overdue.

Winners peaked at +3.05R on average but booked +1.79R — you keep 59% of the run and give back +1.26R. That give-back is large; a further target or a trail is worth testing. Losers sank to -0.44R on average before the stop. 38 of 58 losers first ran to +1.18R in profit before reversing to a full −1R — that is the stop/break-even leak: a break-even or partial rule would have rescued real R on those.

64 trades flagged for a second look — the full list is in the Excel Second Look tab. The lessons this run:

  • 6 winners left money on the table — they peaked at +4.55R on average but you booked far less, handing back +2.95R per trade. This is an exit/target lesson: the runner or a farther TP would recover it. Most concentrated on EURUSD (4t, +1.42R avg).

  • 20 high-score setups still lost (avg score 6.3), bleeding -20.0R. The score did not protect these — consistent with the settled finding that the confidence score does not rank outcomes. Concentrated on EURUSD (7t, -1.00R avg).
