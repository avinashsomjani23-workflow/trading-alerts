Backtest fidelity — same-bar resolutions

108 of 682 resolved trades (16%) exited on the fill bar — the same H1 candle that filled the limit also hit SL (and sometimes TP1). On H1 we cannot tell whether TP or SL printed first, so these are booked SL (pessimistic, no look-ahead). Of the losers, 38 had TP1's price touched in-bar yet booked a full −1R. Read the headline knowing this slice is H1-resolution-limited, not clean directional losses — a lower-timeframe fill model would reclassify some.

Excursion — MFE / MAE (stop & exit tuning)

How far every trade ran in your favour (MFE) and against you (MAE) before it resolved. This is the direct read on whether the stop is too tight, the target too near, or a break-even rule is overdue.

Winners peaked at +2.63R on average but booked +1.64R — you keep 62% of the run and give back +1.00R. That give-back is modest; the current exit captures most of the move. Losers sank to -0.42R on average before the stop. 226 of 372 losers first ran to +1.27R in profit before reversing to a full −1R — that is the stop/break-even leak: a break-even or partial rule would have rescued real R on those.

Trades worth a second look — what the flags mean

407 trades flagged for a second look — the full list is in the Excel Second Look tab. The lessons this run:

  • 37 winners left money on the table — they peaked at +4.42R on average but you booked far less, handing back +2.89R per trade. This is an exit/target lesson: the runner or a farther TP would recover it. Most concentrated on USDCHF (6t, +1.38R avg).
  • 225 losers ran green first — they reached +1.27R in profit on average before reversing to a full loss, bleeding -222.5R in total. 126 of them showed ≥+1R. This is a stop/break-even lesson: a BE or partial rule would have rescued real R. Worst in the Asia (29t, -1.00R avg) bucket.
  • 145 high-score setups still lost (avg score 6.2), bleeding -143.1R. The score did not protect these — consistent with the settled finding that the confidence score does not rank outcomes. Concentrated on USDJPY (20t, -1.00R avg).
